Integrated Wristband Technology for Circadian Rhythm Sleep Disorders

Working in a team of six engineering students, we developed a non-intrusive method of tracking an indiviual's circadian rhythm to help in the diagnosis and research of circadian rhythm sleep disorders (CRSDs). Prior to our project, the majority of diagnosis procedures for CRSDs were either intrusive or unreliable, making it difficult to collect information on CRSDs. We worked closely with our clients, Dr. Tatyana Mollayeva and Dr. Cesar Marquez-Chin, who are scientists at KITE Toronto Rehabilitation Center and the University Health Network (UHN).

I used various project management techniques, namely Gantt charts to monitor and delegate tasks amongst team members, which faciliated with building a strong teamworking environment. From the technnical standpoint, our device utilized event-driven programming to enable a dynamic and user-friendly experience, an internal hard drive to store data, and NFC to transfer data to the doctors for analysis. By the end of our project, we created an integrated wristband prototype that would measure body temperature, heart rate, and bedtime/wake-time, which we introduced in a team showcase to our client.
